# 02 — Principle

The full principle statement lives at [`docs/methodology/02-principle.md`](../../../docs/methodology/02-principle.md) in this repository.

In short: two principles govern the early-stage skills.

1. **Each stage answers only its own destination, leaving the next stage's path blank.** For Select, the destination is the chosen slice's *closed demo line* and its *1–3 Visible Outcomes*. The path — how the slice is implemented — is left to Brainstorming and downstream. After Select, no destination remains; only path refinement.
2. **The AI does not fill blanks by inference.** Within destination scope, only what the user explicitly stated is recorded. Missing values become `TBD: <reason>`, never guessed. For `selecting-slice`, this also means the AI does not propose closed demo lines or Visible Outcomes at all. It may propose candidate slices for naming only when the user explicitly signals being stuck (the B4 fallback) — and even then the user must restate the chosen candidate in their own words.
